Your Body's Pump

The heart works like a pump to send blood all around your body, delivering oxygen and nutrients to every part!

Fun Fact: Your heart beats about 100,000 times every day - that's enough to fill a swimming pool with blood in one year!

Fun Fact: Your heartbeat changes with music. Fast beats speed it up; slow music can slow your heart rate down. Music therapy is real!

Fun Fact: The heart has its own electrical system. It can beat even when disconnected from the body — as long as it has oxygen, it keeps going for a while!

Fun Fact: Octopuses have 3 hearts. Two pump blood to the gills, and one pumps it to the rest of the body. When they swim, the main heart temporarily stops beating.

Heart Chambers:

Left Atrium

Function: Receives oxygen-rich blood from the lungs via the pulmonary veins.

Left Ventricle

Function: Pumps oxygen-rich blood into the aorta, delivering it to the entire body.

Right Atrium

Function: Receives oxygen-poor blood from the body through the superior and inferior vena cava.

Right Ventricle

Function: Pumps oxygen-poor blood into the pulmonary artery, which carries it to the lungs to pick up oxygen.
